📖 أَمْرُ اللَّهِ وَفَرَائِضُهُ

Quranic Foundation of Fara'id (Inheritance)

Islamic inheritance laws are divinely ordained. These specific verses from Surah An-Nisa lay down the precise fractional shares of inheritance (Al-Fara'id).

Surah An-Nisa (4:7) Obligatory Share

لِّلرِّجَالِ نَصِيبٌۭ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ ٱلْوَٰلِدَانِ وَٱلْأَقْرَبُونَ وَلِلنِّسَآءِ نَصِيبٌۭ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ ٱلْوَٰلِدَانِ وَٱلْأَقْرَبُونَ مِمَّا قَلَّ مِنْهُ أَوْ كَثُرَ ۚ نَصِيبًۭا مَّفْرُوضًۭا ٧

"For men there is a share in what their parents and close relatives leave, and for women there is a share in what their parents and close relatives leave—whether it is little or much. ˹These are˺ obligatory shares."

Surah An-Nisa (4:11) Children & Parents

يُوصِيكُمُ ٱللَّهُ فِىٓ أَوْلَـٰدِكُمْ ۖ لِلذَّكَرِ مِثْلُ حَظِّ ٱلْأُنثَيَيْنِ ۚ فَإِن كُنَّ نِسَآءًۭ فَوْقَ ٱثْنَتَيْنِ فَلَهُنَّ ثُلُثَا مَا تَرَكَ ۖ وَإِن كَانَتْ وَٰحِدَةًۭ فَلَهَا ٱلنِّصْفُ ۚ وَلِأَبَوَيْهِ لِكُلِّ وَٰحِدٍۢ مِّنْهُمَا ٱلسُّدُسُ مِمَّا تَرَكَ إِن كَانَ لَهُۥ وَلَدٌۭ ۚ فَإِن لَّمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ وَلَدٌۭ وَوَرِثَهُۥٓ أَبَوَاهُ فَلِأُمِّهِ ٱلثُّلُثُ ۚ فَإِن كَانَ لَهُۥٓ إِخْوَةٌۭ فَلِأُمِّهِ ٱلسُّدُسُ ۚ مِنۢ بَعْدِ وَصِيَّةٍۢ يُوصِى بِهَآ أَوْ دَيْنٍ ۗ ءَابَآؤُكُمْ وَأَبْنَآؤُكُمْ لَا تَدْرُونَ أَيُّهُمْ أَقْرَبُ لَكُمْ نَفْعًۭا ۚ فَرِيضَةًۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ عَلِيمًا حَكِيمًۭا ١١

"Allah commands you regarding your children: the share of the male will be twice that of the female. If you leave only two ˹or more˺ females, their share is two-thirds of the estate. But if there is only one female, her share will be one-half. Each parent is entitled to one-sixth if you leave offspring. But if you are childless and your parents are the only heirs, then your mother will receive one-third. But if you leave siblings, then your mother will receive one-sixth—after the fulfilment of bequests and debts. ˹Be fair to˺ your parents and children, as you do not ˹fully˺ know who is more beneficial to you. ˹This is˺ an obligation from Allah. Surely Allah is All-Knowing, All-Wise."

🔍 Show Verse Explanation & Context
  • ✦ Importance of Learning Al-Fara'id: The knowledge of Islamic inheritance (Al-Fara'id) is derived directly from these three Quranic verses and the corresponding Prophet's Hadiths. Scholars refer to it as "half of knowledge" because it applies universally to all humanity.
  • ✦ Reason for Revelation:
    • Al-Bukhari recorded that Jabir bin `Abdullah became unconscious from severe illness. When the Prophet ﷺ performed ablution and poured water over him, Jabir regained consciousness and asked what to do with his wealth, prompting the revelation of the inheritance laws.
    • In another instance, the wife of Sa`d bin Ar-Rabi` complained that Sa`d's brother took his entire estate after Sa`d was martyred at Uhud, leaving his daughters destitute. The Prophet ﷺ received this verse and ruled: "Give two-thirds of Sa`d's money to his two daughters, one-eighth to their mother, and the remainder is yours."
  • ✦ Rationale for the 2:1 Male-to-Female Ratio: Islamic law mandates that males bear sole financial responsibility for their wives, children, commercial expenses, dowries, and relatives. Females keep their wealth entirely to themselves without mandatory expenditure obligations. Therefore, the male receives a double portion to balance these heavy financial duties.
  • ✦ Proportions for Children: If only daughters survive (two or more), they split two-thirds (2/3) of the estate. A single daughter gets exactly half (1/2).
  • ✦ Proportions for Parents: If children exist, both father and mother get one-sixth (1/6). If childless, the mother gets one-third (1/3) and the father gets the remaining residue (2/3). If siblings exist, the mother's share is reduced to one-sixth (1/6) to offset the father's marital and support expenses for his surviving siblings.
Surah An-Nisa (4:12) Spouses & Kalalah

۞ وَلَكُمْ نِصْفُ مَا تَرَكَ أَزْوَٰجُكُمْ إِن لَّمْ يَكُن لَّهُنَّ وَلَدٌۭ ۚ فَإِن كَانَ لَهُنَّ وَلَدٌۭ فَلَكُمُ ٱلرُّبُعُ مِمَّا تَرَكْنَ ۚ مِنۢ بَعْدِ وَصِيَّةٍۢ يُوصِينَ بِهَآ أَوْ دَيْنٍۢ ۚ وَلَهُنَّ ٱلرُّبُعُ مِمَّا تَرَكْتُمْ إِن لَّمْ يَكُن لَّكُمْ وَلَدٌۭ ۚ فَإِن كَانَ لَكُمْ وَلَدٌۭ فَلَهُنَّ ٱلثُّمُنُ مِمَّا تَرَكْتُم ۚ مِّنۢ بَعْدِ وَصِيَّةٍۢ تُوصُونَ بِهَآ أَوْ دَيْنٍۢ ۗ وَإِن كَانَ رَجُلٌۭ يُورَثُ كَلَـٰلَةً أَوِ ٱمْرَأَةٌۭ وَلَهُۥٓ أَخٌ أَوْ أُخْتٌۭ فَلِكُلِّ وَٰحِدٍۢ مِّنْهُمَا ٱلسُّدُسُ ۚ فَإِن كَانُوٓا۟ أَكْثَرَ مِن ذَٰلِكَ فَهُمْ شُرَكَآءُ فِى ٱلثُّلُثِ ۚ مِنۢ بَعْدِ وَصِيَّةٍۢ يُوصَىٰ بِهَآ أَوْ دَيْنٍ غَيْرَ مُضَآرٍّۢ ۚ وَصِيَّةًۭ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ حَلِيمٌۭ ١٢

"You will inherit half of what your wives leave if they are childless. But if they have children, then ˹your share is˺ one-fourth of the estate—after the fulfilment of bequests and debts. And your wives will inherit one-fourth of what you leave if you are childless. But if you have children, then your wives will receive one-eighth of your estate—after the fulfilment of bequests and debts. And if a man or a woman leaves neither parents nor children but only a brother or a sister ˹from their mother’s side˺, they will each inherit one-sixth, but if they are more than one, they ˹all˺ will share one-third of the estate—after the fulfilment of bequests and debts without harm ˹to the heirs˺. ˹This is˺ a commandment from Allah. And Allah is All-Knowing, Most Forbearing."

🔍 Show Verse Explanation & Context
  • ✦ Spouse Shares:
    • Husband: 1/2 if childless, 1/4 if the wife left children or grandchildren.
    • Wife/Wives: 1/4 if childless, 1/8 if the husband left children or grandchildren. Wives split this share equally.
  • ✦ The Meaning of Kalalah: Kalalah refers to a person who leaves behind neither descendants (offspring/grandchildren) nor ascendants (father/grandfather). Abu Bakr As-Siddiq and other companions defined Kalalah as: "The person who has no children or surviving parents."
  • ✦ Maternal Siblings (from the Mother's side):
    • A single maternal sibling receives one-sixth (1/6).
    • If there are multiple maternal siblings, they share one-third (1/3) of the estate equally, with males and females taking equal shares.
    • They only inherit under the state of Kalalah (in the absence of surviving parents/children).
  • ✦ Estate Priority Settlement: Paying off outstanding debts takes absolute priority over fulfilling bequests and wills, which are limited to a maximum of one-third of the remaining estate.
  • ✦ Free of Harm (Ghayr Mudhar): The verse strictly warns against using bequests (wills) or fabricating fictitious debts to harm heirs or reduce their legitimate shares. Depriving heirs is a grave sin.
Surah An-Nisa (4:13) Ultimate Success

تِلْكَ حُدُودُ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يُطِعِ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ يُدْخِلْهُ جَنَّـٰتٍۢ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَـٰرُ خَـٰلِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ وَذَٰلِكَ ٱلْفَوْزُ ٱلْعَظِيمُ ١٣

"These ˹entitlements˺ are the limits set by Allah. Whoever obeys Allah and His Messenger will be admitted into Gardens under which rivers flow, to stay there forever. That is the ultimate triumph!"
